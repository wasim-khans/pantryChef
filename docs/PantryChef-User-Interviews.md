# PantryChef – User Interviews 🍳

## Interview 1: University Student

**Interviewer**: Hi Alex, thanks for chatting with me. Can you tell me about your cooking situation as a student?

**Alex (21, Computer Science Student)**: Honestly, it's pretty chaotic. I live in a dorm with a tiny kitchenette, mostly just a microwave and mini-fridge. I try to cook sometimes but...

**Interviewer**: What are your biggest challenges when it comes to cooking?

**Alex**: *laughs* Where do I start? I'll buy ingredients for one recipe, use half of them, and the rest just sit in my fridge until they go bad. Last week I threw away a whole bunch of spinach because I only needed a handful for a recipe. I feel so guilty about wasting food and money.

**Interviewer**: So what happens when you're hungry and need to decide what to eat?

**Alex**: Usually I stand in front of my fridge for like 5 minutes going "I have eggs, some cheese, a tomato... what can I even make with this?" Then I either end up making the same boring scrambled eggs again, or I just give up and order pizza. It's expensive and I know it's not healthy.

**Interviewer**: Would a tool that could tell you what to make with your random ingredients help?

**Alex**: Oh my god, yes! If I could just type in "eggs, cheese, tomato" and get actual recipe ideas, that would be a game-changer. I'd probably cook more instead of ordering takeout all the time.

## Interview 2: Busy Professional

**Interviewer**: Sarah, you work long hours as a marketing manager. How does that affect your cooking habits?

**Sarah (32, Marketing Manager)**: It's tough. I get home around 7 PM most days, completely exhausted. The last thing I want to do is stand in the kitchen trying to figure out what to make for dinner.

**Interviewer**: What's your typical dinner routine?

**Sarah**: I open the fridge, stare at the ingredients, feel overwhelmed, and then either make something super basic like pasta with jar sauce, or more likely, just order Uber Eats. I probably spend $300-400 a month on food delivery.

**Interviewer**: Do you feel guilty about that?

**Sarah**: Definitely. I know I'm spending too much money and probably eating less healthy than I should. But after a 10-hour workday, I just don't have the mental energy to plan meals or try new recipes.

**Interviewer**: What if you could search for specific dishes you're craving?

**Sarah**: That would be amazing! Sometimes I'm like "I really want chicken stir-fry" but I don't know exactly what ingredients I need or how to make it properly. If I could just search "chicken stir-fry" and get a simple recipe, I might actually try making it instead of ordering.

## Interview 3: Parent of Two

**Interviewer**: Mike, you have two kids aged 8 and 11. How does meal planning work in your house?

**Mike (38, Software Engineer)**: It's a constant struggle. My kids are picky eaters, my wife and I have different preferences, and we're trying to stick to a budget. Plus, I hate food waste.

**Interviewer**: What does a typical grocery shopping trip look like for you?

**Mike**: I'll buy ingredients with good intentions, like "this week we'll eat healthy!" Then life happens, we don't use everything, and by Friday I'm staring at a fridge full of random vegetables that are about to go bad.

**Interviewer**: Give me an example.

**Mike**: Last week I bought broccoli, carrots, and some chicken. Planned to make stir-fry, but then my daughter had soccer practice, my son didn't feel well, and we ended up ordering pizza. The chicken and veggies sat there until I had to throw them out. Probably $15 worth of food wasted.

**Interviewer**: How would you solve this problem?

**Mike**: If I could quickly input "chicken, broccoli, carrots" and get multiple recipe options, that would be perfect. Especially if it showed different ways to use the same ingredients so we're not eating the same thing every night.

## Interview 4: Health-Conscious Individual

**Interviewer**: Emma, you mentioned you're trying to eat healthier. What are your challenges?

**Emma (26, Yoga Instructor)**: I want to cook more at home because I can control the ingredients and portions, but I'm not a very confident cook. I follow a lot of food bloggers on Instagram, but their recipes seem so complicated.

**Interviewer**: What happens when you try to cook?

**Emma**: I'll see a recipe that looks amazing, buy all these specialty ingredients, make it once, and then the rest of those ingredients just sit in my pantry. I have spices I've used once six months ago.

**Interviewer**: Do you have a fear of cooking?

**Emma**: A little bit, yeah. I'm afraid of messing up, wasting food, or spending time on something that doesn't taste good. So I stick to the same 3-4 basic meals I know I can make.

**Interviewer**: What would help you become more confident?

**Emma**: Simple, clear instructions would be huge. And being able to search by what I already have instead of having to buy new ingredients every time. If I knew I could make something delicious with just the basics in my kitchen, I'd cook way more often.

## Interview 5: Recent Graduate

**Interviewer**: James, you just graduated and started your first job. How's the transition to cooking for yourself?

**James (23, Junior Developer)**: *laughs nervously* It's been rough. In college, I had a meal plan. Now I'm suddenly responsible for all my meals, and I'm realizing I have no idea what I'm doing.

**Interviewer**: What's your biggest struggle?

**James**: I'll go to the grocery store, buy random things that look good, come home, and have no idea how to combine them into actual meals. My fridge is like a collection of unrelated ingredients.

**Interviewer**: Can you give me an example?

**James**: Right now I have ground beef, onions, rice, and some frozen vegetables. I know these probably go together somehow, but I don't know the proportions or cooking times. I'm afraid of messing it up and wasting food.

**Interviewer**: What if you could get help with ingredient combinations?

**James**: That would be incredible. If I could just type in what I have and get actual recipes with step-by-step instructions, I wouldn't feel so lost. I'd probably save so much money on takeout too.

## Common Themes from Interviews

### Problem Patterns
1. **Ingredient Confusion**: "I have X, Y, Z... what can I make?"
2. **Recipe Uncertainty**: "I want to make [dish] but don't know how"
3. **Food Waste Guilt**: "I threw away $X worth of food this week"
4. **Decision Fatigue**: "I'm too tired to decide what to cook"
5. **Budget Concerns**: "I spend too much on takeout"
6. **Cooking Anxiety**: "I'm afraid of messing up"

### Solution Requests
1. **Ingredient-Based Search**: "Type in what I have, get recipes"
2. **Dish-Based Search**: "Search for specific meals I'm craving"
3. **Simple Instructions**: "Clear, step-by-step guidance"
4. **Variety**: "Different ways to use the same ingredients"
5. **Confidence Building**: "Help me become a better cook"

### User Benefits Identified
- Save money by reducing food waste and takeout
- Eat healthier by cooking at home more
- Reduce stress around meal planning
- Build cooking confidence
- Discover new favorite recipes
